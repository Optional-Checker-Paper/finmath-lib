19    /**
20    * This class computes the prices of a collection of call options for a fixed maturity and a family of strikes.
21    *
22    * The pricing method is the FFT methodology as introduced in Carr and Madan (1999).
23    * The transform is taken for -1 &lt; \alpha &lt; 0, hence we have a correction term since we are applying the residue theorem as reported in Lee (2004).
24    * In this strip the transform of any meaningful financial model is well defined because we expect the following conditions to be satisfied:
25    *
26    * 1) When computed in the point z=-i, the discounted characteristic function gives us the initial asset price due to the martingale property.
27    * 2) By definition of characteristic function also z = 0 is a good point.
28    *
29    * Analytic extension over the strip is then guaranteed by Lukacs (1970), Theorem 7.1.1.
30    *
31    * From a financial point of view the choice of this strip corresponds to transforming a covered call position.
32    *
33    * References:
34    * <ul>
35    * <li> Carr. P. and Madan, D. (1999) Option Valuation Using the Fast Fourier Transform. Journal of Computational Finance.</li>
36    * <li> Lee, R. (2004) Option pricing by transform methods: extensions, unification and error control. Journal of Computational Finance.</li>
37    * <li> Lewis, A. (2002) A simple option formula for general jump diffusion and other exponential Levy processes.</li>
38    * <li> Lukacks, E. (1970) Characteristic Functions. 2nd edition.
39    * </ul>

41    * @author Alessandro Gnoatto
42    */
