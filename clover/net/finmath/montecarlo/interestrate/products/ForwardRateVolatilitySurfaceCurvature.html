15    /**
16    * This class implements the calculation of the curvature of the volatility surface of the forward rates.
17    *
18    * <br>
19    *
20    * The value returned by the <code>{@link #getValue(double, TermStructureMonteCarloSimulationModel)}</code> method is calculated as follows:
21    * For each forward rate's instantaneous volatility <i>&sigma;(t)</i> we calculate
22    * \[ \sqrt{ \frac{1}{t_{n} - t_{1}} \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} ( f(t_{i}) )^{2} \cdot (t_{i+1} - t_{i}) } \]
23    * (this is the root mean square / L2 norm of <i>f</i>) where
24    * \[ f(t_{i}) = \frac{ x_{i+2} - 2 x_{i+1} + x_{i} }{ (t_{i+2} - t_{i+1}) (t_{i+1} - t_{i}) } \]
25    * and where
26    * <i>
27    * x<sub>i</sub> = &sigma;<sup>2</sup>(t<sub>i</sub>)
28    * </i> is the
29    * instantaneous variance of a specific forward rate.
30    *
31    * The value returned is then calculated as the average of all those curvatures for all forward rates.
32    *
33    * Note: A tolerance level can be specified. See the documentation of the constructor <code>{@link #ForwardRateVolatilitySurfaceCurvature(double)}</code>.
34    *
35    * <br>
36    * <br>
37    *
38    * While this is not a common financial product, this class can be helpful in calibration procedures, e.g.
39    * to put an additional constrain on the smoothness / curvature of the model surface.
40    *
41    * <br>
42    * A rough interpretation of this product is a follows: If the value 0.25 is returned, then
43    * - on average - the variance of the forward rate oscillates by 0.25 per year, i.e. it moves up by 0.25 and back.
44    * This corresponds to a movement of 0.5 and back per year for the volatility.
45    * Given that you have 100 other calibrations products, and the weight of all (including this one) is 1.0, then
46    * 0.1% deviation of the other calibration products is considered as severe as 0.1 movement per year in the variance (0.3 movement in volatility).
47    * Note: You can control the relation of these by weights.
48    *
49    * @author Christian Fries
50    * @date 12.04.2014.
51    * @version 1.0
52    */
