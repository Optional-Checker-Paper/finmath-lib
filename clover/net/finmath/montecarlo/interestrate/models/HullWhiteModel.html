39    /**
40    * Implements a Hull-White model with time dependent mean reversion speed and time dependent short rate volatility.
41    *
42    * <p>
43    * <b>Model Dynamics</b>
44    * </p>
45    *
46    * The Hull-While model assumes the following dynamic for the short rate:
47    * \[ d r(t) = ( \theta(t) - a(t) r(t) ) d t + \sigma(t) d W(t) \text{,} \quad r(t_{0}) = r_{0} \text{,} \]
48    * where the function \( \theta \) determines the calibration to the initial forward curve,
49    * \( a \) is the mean reversion and \( \sigma \) is the instantaneous volatility.
50    *
51    * The dynamic above is under the equivalent martingale measure corresponding to the numeraire
52    * \[ N(t) = \exp\left( \int_0^t r(\tau) \mathrm{d}\tau \right) \text{.} \]
53    *
54    * The main task of this class is to provide the risk-neutral drift and the volatility to the numerical scheme (given the volatility model), simulating
55    * \( r(t_{i}) \). The class then also provides and the corresponding numeraire and forward rates (LIBORs).
56    *
57    * <p>
58    * <b>Time Discrete Model</b>
59    * </p>
60    *
61    * Assuming piecewise constant coefficients (mean reversion speed \( a \) and short
62    * rate volatility \( \sigma \) the class specifies the drift and factor loadings as
63    * piecewise constant functions for an Euler-scheme.
64    * The class provides the exact Euler step for the joint distribution of
65    * \( (r,N) \), where \( r \) denotes the short rate and \( N \) denotes the
66    * numeraire, following the scheme in <a href="http://ssrn.com/abstract=2737091">ssrn.com/abstract=2737091</a>.
67    *
68    * More specifically (assuming a constant mean reversion speed \( a \) for a moment), considering
69    * \[ \Delta \bar{r}(t_{i}) = \frac{1}{t_{i+1}-t_{i}} \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{i+1}} d r(t) \]
70    * we find from
71    * \[ \exp(-a t) \ \left( \mathrm{d} \left( \exp(a t) r(t) \right) \right) \ = \ a r(t) + \mathrm{d} r(t) \ = \ \theta(t) \mathrm{d}t + \sigma(t) \mathrm{d}W(t) \]
72    * that
73    * \[ \exp(a t_{i+1}) r(t_{i+1}) - \exp(a t_{i}) r(t_{i}) \ = \ \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{i+1}} \left[ \exp(a t) \theta(t) \mathrm{d}t + \exp(a t) \sigma(t) \mathrm{d}W(t) \right] \]
74    * that is
75    * \[ r(t_{i+1}) - r(t_{i}) \ = \ -(1-\exp(-a (t_{i+1}-t_{i})) r(t_{i}) + \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{i+1}} \left[ \exp(-a (t_{i+1}-t)) \theta(t) \mathrm{d}t + \exp(-a (t_{i+1}-t)) \sigma(t) \mathrm{d}W(t) \right] \]
76    *
77    * Assuming piecewise constant \( \sigma \) and \( \theta \), being constant over \( (t_{i},t_{i}+\Delta t_{i}) \), we thus find
78    * \[ r(t_{i+1}) - r(t_{i}) \ = \ \frac{1-\exp(-a \Delta t_{i})}{a \Delta t_{i}} \left( ( \theta(t_{i}) - a \bar{r}(t_{i})) \Delta t_{i} \right) + \sqrt{\frac{1-\exp(-2 a \Delta t_{i})}{2 a \Delta t_{i}}} \sigma(t_{i}) \Delta W(t_{i}) \] .
79    *
80    * In other words, the Euler scheme is exact if the mean reversion \( a \) is replaced by the effective mean reversion
81    * \( \frac{1-\exp(-a \Delta t_{i})}{a \Delta t_{i}} a \) and the volatility is replaced by the
82    * effective volatility \( \sqrt{\frac{1-\exp(-2 a \Delta t_{i})}{2 a \Delta t_{i}}} \sigma(t_{i}) \).
83    *
84    * In the calculations above the mean reversion speed is treated as a constants, but it is straight
85    * forward to see that the same holds for piecewise constant mean reversion speeds, replacing
86    * the expression \( a \ t \) by \( \int_{0}^t a(s) \mathrm{d}s \).
87    *
88    * <p>
89    * <b>Calibration</b>
90    * </p>
91    *
92    * The drift of the short rate is calibrated to the given forward curve using
93    * \[ \theta(t) = \frac{\partial}{\partial T} f(0,t) + a(t) f(0,t) + \phi(t) \text{,} \]
94    * where the function \( f \) denotes the instantanenous forward rate and
95    * \( \phi(t) = \frac{1}{2} a \sigma^{2}(t) B(t)^{2} + \sigma^{2}(t) B(t) \frac{\partial}{\partial t} B(t) \) with \( B(t) = \frac{1-\exp(-a t)}{a} \).
96    *
97    * <p>
98    * <b>Volatility Model</b>
99    * </p>
100    *
101    * The Hull-White model is essentially equivalent to LIBOR Market Model where the forward rate <b>normal</b> volatility \( \sigma(t,T) \) is
102    * given by
103    * \[ \sigma(t,T_{i}) \ = \ (1 + L_{i}(t) (T_{i+1}-T_{i})) \sigma(t) \exp(-a (T_{i}-t)) \frac{1-\exp(-a (T_{i+1}-T_{i}))}{a (T_{i+1}-T_{i})} \]
104    * (where \( \{ T_{i} \} \) is the forward rates tenor time discretization (note that this is the <b>normal</b> volatility, not the <b>log-normal</b> volatility)
105    * (see <a href="http://ssrn.com/abstract=2737091">ssrn.com/abstract=2737091</a> for details on the derivation).
106    * Hence, we interpret both, short rate mean reversion speed and short rate volatility as part of the <i>volatility model</i>.
107    *
108    * The mean reversion speed and the short rate volatility have to be provided to this class via an object implementing
109    * {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.covariance.ShortRateVolatilityModel}.
110    *
111    * This implementation supports different method for the interpolation of the curves.
112    * The property <code>"isInterpolateDiscountFactorsOnLiborPeriodDiscretization"</code> is a boolean. If true, the
113    * given curves are used only at the discretization points given by <code>liborPeriodDiscretization</code>.
114    * This implies that the model reports only a limited set of risk factors in the methods {@link HullWhiteModel#getModelParameters()}.
115    *
116    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.covariance.ShortRateVolatilityModel
117    * @see <a href="http://ssrn.com/abstract=2737091">ssrn.com/abstract=2737091</a>
118    *
119    * @author Christian Fries
120    * @version 1.4
121    */
