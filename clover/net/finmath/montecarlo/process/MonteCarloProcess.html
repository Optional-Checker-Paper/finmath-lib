13    /**
14    * The interface for a process (numerical scheme) of a stochastic process <i>X</i> where
15    * <i>X = f(Y)</i> and Y is an Itô process<br>
16    * \[
17    * dY_{j} = \mu_{j} dt + \lambda_{1,j} dW_{1} + \ldots + \lambda_{m,j} dW_{m}
18    * \]
19    *
20    * The parameters are provided by a model implementing {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.model.ProcessModel}:
21    * <ul>
22    * <li>The value of <i>Y(0)</i> is provided by the method {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.model.ProcessModel#getInitialState}.
23    * <li>The value of &mu; is provided by the method {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.model.ProcessModel#getDrift}.
24    * <li>The value &lambda;<sub>j</sub> is provided by the method {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.model.ProcessModel#getFactorLoading}.
25    * <li>The function <i>f</i> is provided by the method {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.model.ProcessModel#applyStateSpaceTransform}.
26    * </ul>
27    * Here, &mu; and &lambda;<sub>j</sub> may depend on <i>X</i>, which allows to implement stochastic drifts (like in a LIBOR market model)
28    * of local volatility models.
29    *
30    * @author Christian Fries
31    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.model.ProcessModel The definition of the model.
32    * @version 1.0
33    */
