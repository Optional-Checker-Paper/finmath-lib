20    /**
21    * This class implements a <i>Merton Model</i>, that is, it provides the drift and volatility specification
22    * and performs the calculation of the numeraire (consistent with the dynamics, i.e. the drift).
23    *
24    * The model is
25    * \[
26    * dS = \mu S dt + \sigma S dW + S dJ, \quad S(0) = S_{0},
27    * \]
28    * \[
29    * dN = r N dt, \quad N(0) = N_{0},
30    * \]
31    * where \( W \) is Brownian motion and \( J \) is a jump process (compound Poisson process).
32    *
33    * The process \( J \) is given by \( J(t) = \sum_{i=1}^{N(t)} (Y_{i}-1) \), where
34    * \( \log(Y_{i}) \) are i.i.d. normals with mean \( a - \frac{1}{2} b^{2} \) and standard deviation \( b \).
35    * Here \( a \) is the jump size mean and \( b \) is the jump size std. dev.
36    *
37    * The model can be rewritten as \( S = \exp(X) \), where
38    * \[
39    * dX = \mu dt + \sigma dW + dJ^{X}, \quad X(0) = \log(S_{0}),
40    * \]
41    * with
42    * \[
43    * J^{X}(t) = \sum_{i=1}^{N(t)} \log(Y_{i})
44    * \]
45    * with \( \mu = r - \frac{1}{2} \sigma^2 - (exp(a)-1) \lambda \).
46    *
47    * The class provides the model of S to an <code>{@link net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess}</code> via the specification of
48    * \( f = exp \), \( \mu = r - \frac{1}{2} \sigma^2 - (exp(a)-1) \lambda \), \( \lambda_{1,1} = \sigma, \lambda_{1,2} = a - \frac{1}{2} b^2, \lambda_{1,3} = b \), i.e.,
49    * of the SDE
50    * \[
51    * dX = \mu dt + \lambda_{1,1} dW + \lambda_{1,2} dN + \lambda_{1,3} Z dN, \quad X(0) = \log(S_{0}),
52    * \]
53    * with \( S = f(X) \). See {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess} for the notation.
54    *
55    * For an example on the construction of the three factors \( dW \), \( dN \), and \( Z dN \) see {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.assetderivativevaluation.MonteCarloMertonModel}.
56    *
57    * @author Christian Fries
58    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.assetderivativevaluation.MonteCarloMertonModel
59    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess The interface for numerical schemes.
60    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.model.ProcessModel The interface for models provinding parameters to numerical schemes.
61    * @version 1.0
62    */
